The resolution process for a complaint portal typically involves the following steps:

Acknowledgment of Complaint: When a complaint is submitted through a complaint portal, the complainant should receive an acknowledgement that their complaint has been received and is being reviewed.
Investigation: The complaint will then be investigated to determine its validity and the appropriate actions to be taken.
Resolution: Once the investigation is completed, the complaint will be resolved through appropriate actions such as providing a solution, compensation, or apology.
Communication: The complainant should be informed of the resolution process and the outcome of their complaint in a timely manner.
Follow-up: A follow-up may be necessary to ensure that the complainant is satisfied with the resolution of their complaint and to address any further concerns they may have.
Escalation: If the complaint cannot be resolved at the initial level, it may need to be escalated to a higher authority or management for further review and resolution.
Feedback: The feedback received from the complainant can be used to improve the complaint resolution process and to prevent similar complaints from occurring in the future.
It's important to note that the complaint resolution process may vary depending on the specific complaint portal and organization involved.
